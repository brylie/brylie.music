## About This Framework

Mixing is a series of decisions made in a specific order. Getting that order wrong means fixing the same problems repeatedly. This framework organises those decisions into three phases — Production-Integrated, Static Mix, and Dynamic Mix — moving from the broadest strokes to the finest details.

**Phase 0** happens during composition and recording. Gain staging, reference track selection, and mono-checking are not mix tasks; they are production habits that eliminate entire categories of mix problems before they start.

**Phase 1** establishes the static mix: six elements applied in order of impact, from Balance (volume relationships) through Frequency (spectral space), Panorama (stereo width), Dimension (depth), Dynamics (compression), and Interest (early automation). Each element builds on the one before it.

**Phase 2** then revisits every Phase 1 decision over time — turning static choices into dynamic gestures that follow the emotional arc of the piece.
